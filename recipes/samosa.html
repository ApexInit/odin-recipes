Samosa

Description

A samosa is a popular fried or baked pastry snack, particularly in South Asia and the Middle East, filled with savory ingredients like spiced potatoes, peas, and onions.

It's a crispy, golden-brown treat, often enjoyed as a tea-time snack or street food.

Ingredients

for dough:

  • 2 cup maida / plain flour
  • ¼ tsp ajwain / carom seeds
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ cup oil
  • ½ cup water

for stuffing:

  • 2 tsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin / jeera
  • ½ tsp coriander seeds, crushed
  • ½ tsp fennel / saunf
  • pinch hing / asafoetida
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 chilli, finely chopped
  • ½ cup peas / matar
  • ½ tsp kashmiri red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp coriander powder
  • ¼ tsp cumin powder / jeera powder
  • ½ tsp aamchur / dry mango powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ¼ tsp pepper, crushed
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 4 potato / aloo, boiled & mashed
  • 5 cashew / kaju, chopped
  • 2 tbsp raisins / kishmish
  • 2 tbsp coriander, finely chopped

other ingredients:

  • water, for sealing
  • oil, for deep frying

Steps

  1. Take a small portion of dough and lightly coat it with oil.
  2. Roll it into an oval shape using a rolling pin.
  3. Cut the rolled dough horizontally to create two equal halves.
  4. Apply a little water along the edge of one half and shape it into a cone.
  5. Fill the cone with about 2 tablespoons of the prepared potato (aloo) filling.
  6. Dab the open edges with water, press them together, and seal firmly.
  7. To cook, deep-fry the samosas on low heat, turning occasionally, for around 15 minutes until golden and crisp. Alternatively, bake them in a preheated oven at 180°C (356°F) for 40 minutes.
  8. Once done, place the samosas on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  9. Serve hot with green chutney or tamarind chutney for the best experience.

Enjoy!
